Hey Product Hunt! 👋 I'm Scarlett, the maker of Silk Road Knowledge Graph.

**The backstory:** A few weeks ago I visited Dunhuang and stood inside the Mogao Caves. On those thousand-year-old murals, I saw Persian merchants, Indian monks, Roman envoys — all meeting in ONE cave because of ONE road. I came home wanting to understand this road deeply, but found the knowledge scattered across dozens of Wikipedia pages and academic papers. So I built a better way.

**What it is:** An open-source interactive knowledge graph that turns 2,000 years of Silk Road history into something you can actually explore — not just read.

🗺️ **Interactive Map** — 40 cities on real coordinates across Eurasia and the Indian Ocean
🕸️ **Force-Directed Graph** — 326 relationships connecting cities, figures, goods, and events
🎬 **8 Animated Trade Routes** — watch silk, paper, gunpowder, and Buddhism travel across the map
📖 **5 Interactive Stories** — Follow 5 travelers chapter by chapter as the map flies to each location:

| Traveler | Journey | Era |
|----------|---------|------|
| Zhang Qian | Chang'an → Samarkand, the first explorer | 138 BCE |
| Faxian | Chang'an → India → Sri Lanka, at age 60! | 399 CE |
| Xuanzang | The real Tang Monk, 17 years on the road | 629 CE |
| Zheng He | Treasure fleet from Nanjing to East Africa | 1405 CE |
| Marco Polo | Venice → Beijing → back, 24 years | 1271 CE |

**Tech:** Zero frameworks. Pure HTML/CSS/JS. One JSON file is the single source of truth — add one data point and the map, graph, timeline, and stories all update. Hosted on GitHub Pages at zero cost.

**Everything is open source:** Data under CC BY-SA 4.0, code under MIT. 40 cities, 30 trade goods, 23 historical events, 326 edges.

🌐 **Try it:** https://scarlettlab2026.github.io/silk-road-2026
📂 **GitHub:** https://github.com/ScarlettLab2026/silk-road-2026

I'd love your feedback — especially on the interactive stories and the route animations. What other travelers or trade routes should I add next? 🙏
