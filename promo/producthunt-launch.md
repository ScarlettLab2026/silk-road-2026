## Name
Silk Road Knowledge Graph

## Tagline
An open-source interactive map of 2,000 years of East-West exchange — 32 cities, 211 connections, all data-driven

## Description

Explore the Silk Road like never before. We broke down 2,000 years of history into a structured knowledge graph — cities on real coordinates, figures connected through 211 relationship edges, and 8 animated trade routes showing how silk, paper, gunpowder, and Buddhism traveled across continents.

🗺️ Interactive Map (Leaflet.js + CartoDB dark tiles)
🕸️ Force-directed Graph (D3.js v7 — drag to explore connections)
🎬 Route Animations (8 routes with voice narration, generated entirely in Canvas)
🌓 Dual-language (Chinese / English, one-click toggle)
📂 Everything open-source (CC BY-SA 4.0 data + MIT code)

## Maker's Comment

I visited Dunhuang last month and stood in front of the Mogao Caves. On those thousand-year-old murals, I saw merchants from Persia, monks from India, envoys from Rome — all meeting in one cave because of one road.

When I came back, I tried to learn more. I found the knowledge scattered across dozens of Wikipedia pages. To understand Zhang Qian's route, I had to open 10 tabs and reconstruct it in my head. To trace how papermaking reached Europe, I needed 5 different pages.

So I built this. One JSON file. 211 relationships. Every city, figure, trade good, and historical event connected into a living knowledge graph.

The coolest part: the route animations. Select "Paper's Journey West" and watch a golden dot travel from Chang'an to Europe, with narration generated by Web Speech API. 8 routes, 4 minutes, zero editing software — all rendered in Canvas and recorded via MediaRecorder.

If you're into history, data viz, or just want to see what's possible with vanilla JS — I'd love your feedback! The entire project is zero-framework, pure HTML/CSS/JS.

## Data Scale
- 32 Cities (Chang'an → Rome, Quanzhou → Mombasa)

- 22 Trade Goods (silk, paper, porcelain, gunpowder, compass, tea...)
- 16 Historical Events (Zhang Qian's Mission 138 BCE, Battle of Talas 751 CE...)
- 211 Relationship Edges
- 8 Animated Trade Routes
